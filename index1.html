Physical Borrowing

In general, these two charts follow the same pattern. Just like climbing two mountains: the amount arises from September when new academic year starts, till the peak in October, declines in December during the Christmas Holiday, and back to the second peak in March when the summer exams take place. It finally drops to the lowest in summer.
The overall physical borrowing is decreasing every year since 2015. From the blue line of year2019-20, we can conclude that the borrowing amount in this year is the least and it dropped to 0 in April as the Covid-19 Lockdown

Electronic Borrowing

The reason of causing physical borrowing to decline is that the increasing amount of e-usages. The more people are using electronic book, the less physical books have been borrowed
The trend of e-usages is in the opposite direction: the amount keeps rising from 2015 to 2019. The reason of why the 2020 line reacts differently and in a weired pattern, is that the way that e-usage is counted changed during 2020. The data is incomplete.
(The spike in July 2017 is because of some unusual behaviour for one resource)
